Degenerative Retinoschisis

Degenerative Retinoschisis is easily confused with retinal detachment

Degenerative Retinoschisis which does not require treatment of any kind, is easily confused with retinal detachment which requires urgent and high-risk surgery

Examples of Retinoschisis being confused with Retinal Detachments

See inside an example of a 26-year-old male being refered for urgent retinal detachment surgery in both eyes by three retina experts, depiste having perfect vision 20-20 and no symptoms of any kind. He was saved by the bell when the fourth opinion correctly diagnosed Degenerative Retinoschisis

Degenerative Retinoschisis may mimic a retinal detachment

If you are refered for retinal detachment surgery and you don't have any floaters, flashes or curtain, most probably your doctor is confusing retinoschisis with retinal detachment

Even when you have a combined retinoschisis and retinal detachment, NO SURGERY is needed

If you don't have any symptoms or symptoms are minimal you might only need observation
